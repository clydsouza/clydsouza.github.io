Welcome to my website! I'm Clyde D'Souza, a first-year undergraduate student at the University of Connecticut. I'm currently pursuing dual degrees in Molecular and Cell Biology and Applied Mathematics, with minors in Chemistry and English.

When I'm not in class or doing homework, I enjoy biking, leading future Huskies as a Husky Ambassador, or writing for our undergraduate newspaper, the Daily Campus. As a campus correspondent for the Life section, I regularly write articles about events and media throughout campus and the broader internet

At the moment I work part time as an undergraduate research assistant in the Biobehavioral Research Lab under Dr. Ruth Lucas, in the UConn School of Nursing. Additionally, I am a mathematics tutor at the Quantitative Learning Center, where I help teach Calculus I, II and III.

I'm also heavily involved in on and off-campus research. Throughout my first year at UConn, I assisted as an undergraduate research assistant in the Oral Drug Delivery Lab of Dr. Na Li, in the UConn School of Pharmacy, where I engaged in ongoing research on the diffusion dynamics of nanoparticles and amorphous solid dispersions. I have recently been accepted to the Health Research Program for the summer of 2024, where I am excited to work under Dr. Ephraim Trakhtenberg in the exciting field of neuronal regeneration.

I am not exactly sure where my trajectory will land me in the future, but I see my self at the intersection of medicine and bioinformatics research. To be continued!
